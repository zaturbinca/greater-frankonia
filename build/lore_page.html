The Frankonian Saga

Our history diverges. Our destiny is forged in steel and strategy. Understand the world of Frankonia.

Alternate History: 1946

Following the Great War, Germany, having not surrendered despite the loss of Alsace-Lorraine, negotiated an armistice devoid of punitive restrictions. The sole concession was a territorial adjustment, reshaping Germany to its modern-day borders. Crucially, the Treaty of Versailles , with its crippling reparations and military limitations, was never enacted.

This absence of constraint propelled Germany into an unprecedented industrial boom . The Rhineland, already a key industrial area, transformed into the most heavily industrialized region in all of Europe, becoming the engine of German prosperity. The nation thrived, re-establishing itself as a major global power.

However, this era of prosperity was shattered in 1935. Amidst the fervor of the industrial expansion, the region of Frankonia, fueled by its newfound economic power and distinct identity, declared independence from Germany. This bold act ignited a brutal civil war that would rage for over a decade, dragging on into the late 1940s.

Our timeline is set in the year 1946. The conflict persists, and the current operational area depicted on the map is located within the heavily industrialized Rhineland region, specifically in territories recently conquered or contested by Frankonian forces.

Key Lore Points

  • Ideology: Monarchy
  • National Animal: Bear
  • Setting: Industrialized Rhineland, 1946
  • Conflict: Ongoing Civil War against Germany

The Battlefield Layout

The primary operational zone is a designated district within the contested Rhineland region. Its layout is strategically designed for defense and control:

  • The Base: A massive fortification dominates the landscape, featuring formidable walls and defined borders. This serves as the central Frankonian stronghold.
  • The Industrial City: Situated directly outside the base's perimeter is a bustling industrial city. This area is home to the civilian population and vital production facilities.
  • The Rebel Town: Further out, beyond the city limits, lies a smaller town. This serves as the primary spawning and staging area for the rebel forces.
  • Headquarters & Government: Located inside the main base, there is an inner fortification housing the central headquarters, government buildings, and key command structures.

Control of these key areas dictates the flow and outcome of engagements.
