Rank Privileges

  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] [ROYAL FAMILY] Grand Marshal: No new privileges.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Field Marshal: No new privileges.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Lieutenant General: No new privileges.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Major General: Authorized to host SSUs.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Brigadier General: Authorized to rank personnel within the Armed Forces and co-host SSUs.
  • [LOWER HIGH COMMAND] Colonel: Authorized to rank personnel within their specific regiment.
  • [LOWER HIGH COMMAND] Lt. Colonel: No new privileges.
  • [OFFICER] Major: No new privileges.
  • [OFFICER] Captain: No new privileges.
  • [OFFICER] 1st Lieutenant: Authorized to host training sessions.
  • [OFFICER] 2nd Lieutenant: No new privileges.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Sergeant Major: Authorized to co-host training sessions.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Sergeant First Class: No new privileges.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Sergeant: Authorized to operate any equipment or station at the border without direct supervision.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Corporal: No new privileges.
  • [ENLISTED] Lance Corporal: Authorized to operate a specific line or station at the border under supervision.
  • [ENLISTED] Private First Class: No new privileges.
  • [ENLISTED] Private: The lowest ranking border guard, not authorized to operate border equipment or stations.
  • [ENLISTED] Enlist: Entry-level rank. Required to complete initial training. Permitted to guard the border when training is not being hosted.

Note: The Royal Guard division is supervised directly by the Emperor, Crowned Prince, and Royal Advisor, with a Director appointed to manage the division's day-to-day operations.

Key Responsibilities

  • Chancellery: Head of Government, oversees all civil matters and policy.
  • Secretariat: Manages specific departments (e.g., Foreign Affairs, Treasury).
  • Council: Handles legislation, record-keeping, and policy implementation.
  • Civil Service: Entry-level administrative tasks and data collection.
