Military Responsibilities

Key duties and roles for each rank.

  • Grand Marshal: The supreme overseer of the armed forces, ensuring the efficiency of the military leadership (Field Marshal and Generals). (Only one position).
  • Field Marshal: The primary director of the armed forces, responsible for leading overall operations, advised by the Lieutenant and Major Generals. (Only one position).
  • Lieutenant General: Oversees all regiments and works directly with the Field Marshal in directing the armed forces. (Only one position).
  • Major General: Oversees a specific regiment (Military Police, Special Forces, or Mechanized Infantry), ensuring its smooth operation. Advises the Lieutenant General and Field Marshal on overall military direction. (Three positions, one per specified regiment).
  • Brigadier General: Works alongside the Colonel to direct the operations of a regiment. More of an overseer position.
  • Colonel: The primary commander of a regiment, responsible for its overall administration and tasks (including recruitment and assignments). Works with the Brigadier General, who assists with direction, particularly for larger operations and bigger decisions.
  • Lieutenant Colonel: The second-in-command (Executive Officer) of a regiment, assisting the Colonel with regimental duties but with less overall authority.
  • Major: Oversees a squad, acting as a commander at the squad level, similar to a Colonel for a regiment.
  • Captain: Leads a squad, similar to a Lieutenant Colonel for a regiment.

Military Privileges

Specific authorities granted at each rank.

  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] [ROYAL FAMILY] Grand Marshall: No new privileges.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Field Marshall: No new privileges.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Lieutenant General: No new privileges.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Major General: Authorized to host SSUs.
  • [UPPER HIGH COMMAND] Brigadier General: Authorized to rank personnel within the Armed Forces and co-host SSUs.
  • [LOWER HIGH COMMAND] Colonel: Authorized to rank personnel within their specific regiment.
  • [LOWER HIGH COMMAND] Lt. Colonel: No new privileges.
  • [OFFICER] Major: No new privileges.
  • [OFFICER] Captain: No new privileges.
  • [OFFICER] 1st Lieutenant: Authorized to host training sessions.
  • [OFFICER] 2nd Lieutenant: No new privileges.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Sergeant Major: Authorized to co-host training sessions.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Sergeant First Class: No new privileges.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Sergeant: Authorized to operate any equipment or station at the border without direct supervision.
  • [NON COMMISSIONED OFFICER] Corporal: No new privileges.
  • [ENLISTED] Lance Corporal: Authorized to operate a specific line or station at the border under supervision.
  • [ENLISTED] Private First Class: No new privileges.
  • [ENLISTED] Private: The lowest ranking border guard; not authorized to operate border equipment or stations.
  • [ENLISTED] Enlist: Entry-level rank. Required to complete initial training. Permitted to guard the border when training is not being hosted.

Note: The Royal Guard division is supervised directly by the Emperor, Crowned Prince, and Royal Advisor, with a Director appointed to manage the division's day-to-day operations.
