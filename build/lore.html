The Frankonian Saga

Our history diverges. Our destiny is forged in steel and strategy. Understand the world of Frankonia.

Alternate History: 1946

In this timeline, World War I concludes not with a decisive victory, but with a weary armistice in 1918. The infamous Treaty of Versailles is never signed. Germany, though battered, remains intact and transitions into the Weimar Republic. The subsequent decades are marked by political instability and economic struggle, but without the crushing weight of war reparations, the nation avoids the hyperinflation and extremist ideologies that plagued our own history. World War II, as we know it, never occurs.

Instead, Germany experiences a slow, fragmented recovery. The 1930s see a massive industrial boom, heavily concentrated in the resource-rich Rhineland. This creates a vast economic and cultural divide between the prosperous west and the struggling east. The Rhineland develops a unique identity, fiercely independent and proud of its economic power. Calls for autonomy grow louder, fueled by a resurgence of monarchist sentiment that idealizes the old German empires.

By 1945, the political situation reaches a boiling point. The Rhineland, under the leadership of the charismatic Rheinhold royal family, declares independence, forming the Germanic Empire of Frankonia. The central German government in Berlin refuses to recognize this secession, branding the Frankonians as rebels. Diplomatic efforts fail, and by early 1946, the situation escalates into a full-blown civil war.

Frankonia, with its industrial might and a highly motivated, disciplined military, establishes a formidable defensive line. The conflict becomes a brutal war of attrition, fought in the cities, forests, and factory complexes of the Rhineland. It is now 1946, and the war rages on. The future of Frankonia, and indeed Germany, will be decided by the valor of its soldiers and the strength of its convictions.

The Battlefield Layout

The primary operational zone is a designated district within the contested Rhineland region. Its layout is strategically designed for defense and control:

  • The Base: A massive fortification dominates the landscape, featuring formidable walls and defined borders. This serves as the central Frankonian stronghold.
  • The Industrial City: Situated directly outside the base's perimeter is a bustling industrial city. This area is home to the civilian population and vital production facilities.
  • The Rebel Town: Further out, beyond the city limits, lies a smaller town. This serves as the primary spawning and staging area for the rebel forces.
  • Headquarters & Government: Located inside the main base, there is an inner fortification housing the central headquarters, government buildings, and key command structures.

Control of these key areas dictates the flow and outcome of engagements.
